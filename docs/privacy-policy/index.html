Privacy Policy for PureSticker

Last updated: May 24, 2024

PureSticker ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how our mobile application (the "App") collects, uses, and safeguards your information.

By using the App, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy.

1. Information Collection and Use

PureSticker works primarily offline. We do not collect, store, or transmit your personal data to our own servers. All sticker packs, images, and metadata created within the App are stored locally on your device.

a. User-Generated Content

The App allows you to create sticker packs using images and videos from your device. This content is stored in your device's local database using Android's Room library. We do not have access to your photos or created stickers.

b. Personal Information (Metadata)

When creating a sticker pack, you may optionally provide an email address, website, or author name. This information is saved strictly as metadata within the sticker pack file structure on your device. It is required by the WhatsApp Sticker format to attribute authorship but is not collected, analyzed, or sent to us.

2. Permissions and Device Access

To function correctly, the App requests the following permissions:

  • Read Storage (Images/Video): Required to allow you to select photos and videos from your gallery to convert them into stickers.

Note: We verify WhatsApp installation using specific package queries defined in the app manifest, respecting user privacy by avoiding broad package visibility permissions.

3. Third-Party Services and Data Sharing

We do not sell or share your data with third parties. Data is only shared in the following specific scenario initiated by you:

a. WhatsApp

When you choose to "Add to WhatsApp," the App exports the selected sticker pack data (images and metadata) directly to the WhatsApp application installed on your device via Android's Content Provider mechanism. This interaction is governed by WhatsApp's own privacy policy.

b. Google ML Kit

We use Google ML Kit for on-device machine learning to assist with image processing (e.g., removing backgrounds from selfies). Processing happens locally on your device, and no image data is sent to Google servers for this purpose.
